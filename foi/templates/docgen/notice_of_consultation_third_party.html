What is the FOI Act?

The FOI Act grants every person a legal right to request access to documents in the possession of agencies of the Australian Government. When a document is released to someone under FOI, there are no restrictions on what the recipient may do with the document (other than acting lawfully, for example, by not breaching copyright in the document).

Are there any limits on disclosure?

The FOI Act contains a number of provisions that provide an exemption from release of documents under a range of circumstances.

Conditional Exemptions:

The following "conditional exemptions" may apply to the document or documents. A conditionally exempt document is not exempt from release unless it would also, on balance, be contrary to the public interest to release it.

Factors favouring access include (but are not limited to): • Promoting the objects of the FOI Act (a copy of the objects clause (section 3) is enclosed for your information); • Informing debate on a matter of public importance; • Promoting effective oversight of public expenditure; and • Allowing a person access to his or her own personal information.

Arguments against release need to counter-balance these considerations.

{% if assessment.include_s47b_flag = True %}

section 47B:

One of these provisions, section 47B, includes a conditional exemption from release for certain documents that contain information originating with, or received from, a State or an authority of a State. In order to determine whether this provision applies to these documents, the agency has to establish that disclosure:

a) would or could reasonably be expected to cause damage to relations between the Commonwealth and a State; or

b) would divulge information or matter communicated in confidence by or on behalf of the Government of a State or an authority of the State, to the Government of the Commonwealth, to an authority of the Commonwealth or to a person receiving the communication on behalf of the Commonwealth or an authority of the Commonwealth.

{% elif assessment.include_s47f_flag = True %}

section 47F:

One of these provisions, section 47F, includes a conditional exemption from release for documents that contain personal information which it would be unreasonable to disclose. In order to determine whether this provision applies to these documents, the agency has to establish that disclosure would:

a) involve the unreasonable disclosure of personal information. Section 4 of the FOI Act defines personal information as information or an opinion about an identifiable individual; and

b) be unreasonable having regard to the following matters: • the extent to which the information is well known; • whether the person to whom the information relates is known to be (or to have been) associated with the matters dealt with in the document; • the availability of the information from publicly accessible sources; • any other matter that the agency or Minister considers relevant.

{% elif assessment.include_s47g_flag = True %}

section 47G:

One of these provisions, section 47G, includes a conditional exemption from release for documents that contain information concerning a person in respect of his or her business or professional affairs or concerning the business, commercial or financial affairs of an organisation or undertaking, where disclosure:

a) would, or could reasonable be expected to, unreasonably affect that person adversely in respect to his or her lawful business or professional affairs or that organisation or undertaking in respect of its lawful business, commercial or financial affairs; or

b) could reasonably be expected to prejudice the future supply of information to the Department for the purpose of administration of a law of the Commonwealth or a Territory or the administration of matters administered by an agency.

Publication

The department must publish information that has been released to an applicant in response to a freedom of information request, subject to certain exceptions. This publication is known as a disclosure log. The disclosure log requirement does not apply to personal information about any person if it would be unreasonable to publish the information nor to information about the business, commercial, financial or professional affairs of any person if publication of that information would be unreasonable. You might also like to express a view regarding the publication of any information which you consider it would be unreasonable to publish. Please note that the Department is not required to consult you further on any decision to publish information released to the FOI applicant. The decision to publish information is not subject to review either by the Department or the Australian Information Commissioner. It is however, open to any person to make a complaint to the Australian Information Commissioner about how an agency handles an FOI request.

You should be aware that the disclosure log facilitates publication to the world at large of information released under FOI to individual applicants. This reinforces the objective of the FOI Act to promote a pro-disclosure culture across government and to increase recognition that information held by government is a national resource.
